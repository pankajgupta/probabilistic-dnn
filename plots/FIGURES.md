# Figures

- **fig1_activation_deformation.png** -- Measured p-neuron firing probability vs the IST-predicted CDF for six randomness defects plus two temporal negative controls (AR(1) rho=0.9, LFSR-8) that match the ideal curve as expected. _(source: results/e1_activation.json)_
- **fig2_precision_tolerance.png** -- Accuracy vs RNG bit-depth k for S in {1,4,32}: no knee down to k=2, and even k=1 loses under half a point at S=32 relative to full precision. _(source: results/e2_precision.json)_
- **fig3_bias.png** -- Accuracy at S=1/32 vs bias b (top) and frustrated-triangle TV distance vs the same bias axis (bottom, shared x): both metrics degrade monotonically with bias. _(source: results/e2_precision.json, results/e4_cross_task.json)_
- **fig4_headline_sampling_fragility.png** -- THE HEADLINE: accuracy vs number of inference samples S for ideal, rho=0.9/0.99/1.0 correlated noise, and k=2-independent noise -- independence (even at 2-bit precision) recovers the sampling gain that correlation removes. _(source: results/e3_sampling.json, results/e2_precision.json)_
- **fig5_correlation_grid.png** -- Left: accuracy heatmap over the full correlation-strength x sample-count grid. Right: sharing one noise draw across neurons (same forward pass) costs accuracy at S=1 but the multi-sample gain still recovers -- a different failure mode from across-sample correlation. _(source: results/e3_sampling.json)_
- **fig6_ece.png** -- ECE vs S for ideal, k=2-independent, rho=0.9, rho=1.0: ECE rises with S under ideal/independent noise (the net becomes underconfident as it averages), while rho=1.0 stays flat and ends up with LOWER measured ECE than ideal at large S. _(source: results/e3_ece.json)_
- **fig7_cross_task.png** -- H4 cross-task overlay: triangle TV distance (top, log y) and MNIST accuracy drop at S=32 relative to each family's least-defective point (bottom), for bit-depth, bias, and correlation. LFSR-8 is annotated as a near-ideal MNIST point that is ~82x ideal TV distance on the triangle. _(source: results/e4_cross_task.json, results/e2_precision.json, results/e3_sampling.json)_
- **fig8_ste_robustness.png** -- Frozen (add-noise-to-trained-model) vs STE-trained (S_train=4, faded S_train=1) accuracy vs S under ideal, bias, and correlated-noise conditions: STE's edge is largest at S=1 and most decisive when rho=1.0 removes the multi-sample gain. _(source: results/e5_ste.json)_
- **fig9_triangle_validation.png** -- Appendix: exact vs empirical frustrated-triangle state probabilities under ideal RNG (left), and a pass/fail summary of period / lag-1 autocorrelation / chi-square histogram tests for each RNG source (right). _(source: results/e4_cross_task.json, results/rng_stats.json)_
